Our Mission

Our mission is to empower students, particularly those who have experienced significant adversity, including involvement or risk of involvement in child welfare and/or other social service systems with the requisite academic, social, emotional, and life skills to be successful in college, career, and community. In addition, we aim to create an outstanding school that attracts, supports, and retains exceptional and caring people.

Academics

Monument Academy's academic programs are centered on thematic planning, supported by exposure through relevant field experiences, project-based learning enriched in principles of Webb's Depth of Knowledge, higher-order questioning as rooted in Bloom's Taxonomy, and robust writing across curriculum with the use of the Collins Writing Program to target individual student needs.

Each Monument Academy student engages in a targeted learning journey that is rooted in data driven instruction as evidenced by triangulation of NWEA Measures of Academic (MAP) data (implemented 3x a year), quarterly assessments, and teacher-made assessments. In addition, qualitative data is captured through monthly learning walks, weekly collaborative planning sessions, and teacher feedback We seek to develop self-directed learners by engaging students in academic goal-setting during student led conferences, and structuring an environment that provides options for how students access content, practice new skills, and demonstrate mastery of the standards using technology and research.

Family-Style

Students board at Monument Academy from Sunday evening through to Friday afternoon. They live in small, family-style single gender homes, with two Student Life Advisors for each home of 10 students.

Students receive a personal wake-up and eat a family-style breakfast every morning with their Student Life Advisors and Student Life Family. Students share a personal daily goal with their family and head to advisory for community building.

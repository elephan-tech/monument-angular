Life skills are essential to students’ long-term success. We embed explicit life skills development throughout our day to ensure ongoing opportunities for learning, practice, and application.

Independent living skills are critical for student success, particularly once they graduate from high school and continue on to college, a career and life in the community. We know from research that these skills can’t just be taught in the classroom. They must be developed from years of modeling and practice, as they require the same type of instruction and ample practice time as learning any other academic or social emotional skills.

How do we teach life skills at Monument? In addition to our academic program, our weekday boarding program provides us with continuous opportunities for teaching important life skills, such as personal hygiene, cooking, routine home maintenance and repairs, financial literacy, time management, and accessing community resources.
